Violinist Shoji Tabuchi was born on April 16, 1944 in Japan (He dies at the age of 79, on August 11, 2023).

Fiddle musician and singer of Japanese descent who specializes in country music. In 1990, he opened The Violinist Shoji Tabuchi Theatre in Branson, Missouri, as his own performing venue.

He studied classical violin as a child in Japan, but his true love was American country music. During his college years, he formed The Bluegrass Ramblers, which went on to win a national music competition in Japan.

To remember his son, Thomas Jason Lingo-Tabuchi, who died at the age of nineteen, he founded a music scholarship.

He was born and reared in the Japanese city of Ishikawa. Later, he and his first wife, Mary Jo, immigrated to the United States and resided in Missouri. In 1980, he married his second wife, Dorothy Lingo.

He attended and was highly impressed by a Grand Ole Opry star Country Singer Roy Acuff concert in Osaka, Japan when he was a young guy.

Take a look inside Tottenham's new stadium for the first time, including the UK's longest bar

TAKE a look inside the incredible features of Tottenham's new stadium for the first time, including the UK's longest bar.

Spurs' new ground is set to feature the "Goal Line Bar", which runs the entire width of the pitch, spanning an incredible 65m.

On top of that, Tottenham will introduce the "Market Place", a street-food inspired emporium, as well as luxury TVs scattered around the inside of the ground.

The new stadium will also boast a plush club shop - as well as the much-publicised £9,500-a-season "Tunnel Club".

Tottenham still hope the new ground will be ready for the 2018-19 season - after releasing the astronomical costs of a season ticket earlier this week.

Spurs are currently playing at Wembley for all of their home games as their £750million ground is built - although some reports suggest the cost is climbing to closer to £1BILLION.

The 62,000-seater ground will have a retractable pitch for up to 16 non-football events a year, including concerts and NFL games.

And the largest single-tiered end in Europe — bigger even than Borussia Dortmund’s "Yellow Wall" — will accommodate 17,000 home fans behind one goal.

As well as the pricey Tunnel Club, Spurs' money-spinning new stadium will feature a host of other upmarket season tickets.

The H Club, with its Michelin-starred chefs and cheese waiters will set you back £15,000 per season, which Spurs are quick to point out is less than the Diamond Club at Arsenal’s Emirates.

Tickets for the Tunnel Club and The H Club come in pairs — and to be eligible to buy those, you must first stump up £30,000 for a couple of memberships.

In fact, Spurs expect to generate an additional £28m-a-year from match-days at their new home, a rise of over 50 per cent - with Daniel Levy revealing they have received expressions of interest from credible counterparties" for naming rights to the ground.

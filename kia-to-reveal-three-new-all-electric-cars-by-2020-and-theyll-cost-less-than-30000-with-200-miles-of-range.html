Kia to reveal THREE new all-electric cars by 2020 - and they'll cost less than 30,000 with 200 mil

BY the end of this paragraph you’ll know that Kia will launch three all-electric cars in the next 18 months – all capable of 200 miles-plus and costing less than £30,000.

You’ll also know there’s a Sportage hybrid coming in September.

Nissan. This is your wake-up call.

In fact, Ford, Vauxhall, VW, Toyota, all the major mainstream players. This is your wake-up call.

Kia is going to be a serious contender in the switch to electric.

The zero-emission Niro you see here goes on sale in November with a 64kWh battery and a 238-mile range. Kia promises a 115-mile top-up in 30 minutes with a fast charger.

That’s a real poke in the eye for the new 40kWh Nissan Leaf (180 miles). Even the yet-to-be-confirmed-but-it’s-coming 60kWh Leaf is smaller.

Kia’s next-gen Soul EV follows in January, sharing the same battery as the Niro. Then we’ll see an all-electric Stonic.

Petrol versions of the big-selling Sportage SUV will be offered with a 48-volt mild hybrid from September.

First thing to note: No messy leads. It sets off in complete silence, using only electric power before the engine kicks in, and the battery regenerates as you drive.

It also means more mpg and lower emissions. That’s another poke in the eye for Nissan. There’s no Qashqai hybrid yet. In all, Kia has promised 16 electrified vehicles by 2025 – five pure electric, five mild hybrids, five plug-in hybrids and a fuel-cell electric vehicle.

So you can expect Kia’s next new car, the cee’d coming this summer, to have pure electric and hybrid versions in the next few years too.

Kia chief Woong-chul Yang, speaking at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas, also revealed plans for 5G in-car connectivity, hand-gesture controls, face recognition and different “sound zones” where front and rear passengers can listen to their own music.

He said: “We are taking a new leap forward.”

You’re not wrong, Mr Woong.

At affordable prices.

And with a seven-year warranty.

People are putting plant pots over candles to heat their homes - and its keeping them super cosy

WITH bills rising this year, people are getting creative with finding ways to heat their homes. 

People are taking to TikTok to share how they are placing plant pots over candles to make rooms warmer - but experts have warned against the trend.

TikTok user Marie Smith, who posts under @mas_2903, showed how she got three B&M candles which were priced at £2 each.

After lighting them, she then got a terracotta plant pot and placed it on top.

The theory is that the pot traps and absorbs heat from the candle, and acts like a mini radiator.

Marie explained why she made the DIY heater, saying: “Our heating system is so old it takes twice as long to heat up and costs loads!

“We are buying a new boiler and all new plumbing.

“Kept us warm for the day.”

She added that she constantly watched it and didn’t leave it unattended.

Although some people were impressed, others pointed out that it could be a fire hazard.

And an assistant commissioner at the London Fire Brigade agreed saying the terracotta heaters can be very dangerous.

The Brigade’s Assistant Commissioner Charlie Pugsley said: “We understand people are concerned about the cost of living and are looking for cheaper ways to heat their homes.

“But as firefighters, we do sadly see the devastating results of when things go wrong and we have to highlight the risks involved.

“Candles are one of the most common causes of fires in the home and they shouldn’t be used for anything other than their intended purpose. Although life hacks can be handy, so many of them have the potential to be dangerous, and we would urge people not to follow videos online if they could put you or others in danger.

“We would always recommend people swap to using safer LED candles rather than flame ones, but if you are using them they should never be left unattended.

“It’s also important people have working smoke alarms and know what to do in the event of the fire. For tips on how to keep safe from fire, take a look at the Brigade’sHome Fire Safety Checker which will give you advice tailored for your home.

“If you’re struggling to pay your energy bills, Citizens Advice can give you information on grants and benefits that can help you.”

THE FIRE BRIGADE'S ADVICE FOR CANDLE SAFETY

• Candles need to be used in a safe, fire resistant holder or container – as you can see in the video, the flower pot would only need the slightest knock to cause it and the candles to fall.

• Candles need to be supervised at all times – so blow them out if you need to leave the room.

• Don’t cover candles with anything – anything placed over a candle flame will get very hot, and could melt or catch fire.

• Stick to radiators and if you’re especially cold, a hot water bottle is a much safer alternative.

Many people expressed their sadness that the pots were needed at all, with one saying: “we shouldn't have to live like this.”

With energy prices still giving us the shivers and bills rising, now's the time to find other ways to keep warm.

Martin shared the Money Saving Expert (MSE) guide, heat the human not the home, to help vulnerable households stay warm without raising bills.

The key is to keep yourself warm rather than pumping hot air into your house. These low-cost ideas should help you save on your bills.

Naturally, the more clothes you have on, the warmer you should feel.

You can wear as many layers as feels comfortable for you, but aim for a minimum of two when indoors and three when outdoors in cold conditions.

Wearing heated clothing is another idea, from fingerless gloves to warming gilets that either have a battery pack or a USB plug in.

After you've wrapped your body up, make sure you put something on your feet. Walking around the house barefoot can counteract your others attempts to warm up.

It's as simple as putting on a pair of socks or using your slippers around the house.

Airbnb cabin owner 'secretly took 2,000 videos of guests naked and having sex on hidden camera disgu

A HOTEL owner allegedly took more than 2,000 videos and images of guests naked and having sex on a hidden camera.

The device was found inside one of the rooms of Airbnb host A Jay Allee’s secluded Texas cabin and was apparently made to look like a power adapter for a router, police said.

The 54-year-old faces 15 counts of invasive visual recording after lawsuits accused him of invasion of privacy and intentionally inflicting emotional distress.

His lawyer says he’s innocent of all the charges, KSAT reported.  

The camera, which cost less than $130, was found by a couple staying at the retreat, located in Comfort, in July last year.

It had reportedly been in place for nine months.

While a woman was showering, a man claimed he spotted the charger facing the bed, according to an arrest warrant affidavit.

It claims that the couple felt uncomfortable when Allee allegedly told them to watch the sunrise in the pajamas or nude.

The renter searched the cabin to see if there was anything unusual and googled how to find hidden devices.

Kendall County cops launched a probe and unearthed that he had allegedly filmed guests for at least a year, the San Antonio Express-News reported.

Police found the camera and the router that was attached to it and seized laptops, phones, and a tablet.

In November last year, cops discovered at least 2,100 thumbnail images of men and women inside the cabin.

The affidavit claimed that some of them were naked, partially undressed, and involved in sexual conduct.

'REAL-LIFE SCARY MOVIE'

Attorney Bianca Zuniga-Goldwater, who represents 17 people that stayed at the cabin, said: “It’s a real-life scary movie."

Allee was initially arrested last November and charged with four counts of invasive visual recording.

He now faces a total of 15 counts after extra charges were filed.

Nico LaHood, who is part of the host’s defense team, says Allee maintains his innocence.

The attorney said: “He is looking forward to his day in court."

LaHood says he told his client “it’s a marathon, not a sprint”.

A civil suit filed in December revealed that Allee was both an Airbnb superhost and a VRBO premier host.

Airbnb told KSAT: “On our platform, we strictly ban hidden cameras.

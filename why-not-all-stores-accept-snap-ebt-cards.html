Not all grocery stores accept SNAP EBT cards - here's why

AMERICANS who are part of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) have a variety of choices of where to use their benefits, but it also comes with restrictions.

SNAP is not accepted everywhere.

The program, once known as food stamps, deposits a monthly stipend onto a beneficiary's electronic benefit transfer (EBT) card, which is used like a debit card.

You can use your EBT card to buy groceries at authorized food stores and retailers.

You need to look for signage that states which retailers accept SNAP EBT cards.

If there is no signage, you will typically find yourself denied at checkout.

Why not all grocery stores accept SNAP

When a participant shops at a SNAP authorized retail store, their SNAP EBT account is debited to reimburse the store for food that was purchased.

Retailers need to be approved by the US Department of Agriculture (USDA) which oversees SNAP.

These retailers have their own application guidelines to follow, as well.

Retailers must apply for a SNAP permit.

This permit is only good for the location and owner on record.

Each store location must be SNAP authorized.

If an owner sells the store, the new owner may not use the prior owner's SNAP permit.

Also, SNAP retailers must buy or lease their own EBT equipment.

SNAP licensed retailers may not charge state or local sales tax on SNAP purchases.

All SNAP retailers must go through training.

Then, every five years the retailer will need to get reauthorized.

What are the qualifications to become a SNAP retailer?

SNAP authorized stores must meet one of two staple food requirements:

Staple food inventory

A store must have three stocking units of three different varieties for each staple food category on a continuous basis.

For two staple food categories, there must be at least one perishable variety.

Most stores are authorized under this criteria.

Staple food sales

A store must have more than 50% of its total gross retail sales from the sale of staple foods.

Specialty stores, like butcher shops, are often authorized under requirement.

Staple foods are the basic foods that make up a significant portion of a personâ€™s diet. They are usually prepared at home and eaten as a meal.

This does not include prepared foods or heated foods.

Staple food categories include: vegetables or fruit, dairy products, meat, poultry or fish, and breads or cereals.

How do you qualify for SNAP?

Throughout the US, more than 41.5million Americans receive SNAP benefits every month.

You must apply for SNAP in the state in which you live and meet certain bank balance limits.

The total amount of SNAP benefits your household gets each month is called an allotment.

SNAP households are expected to spend about 30% of their own resources on food.

The USDA says the maximum monthly allotment is based on household size. 

For example, for a family of four, the maximum allotment is $835.

Each state has a different application form and process.

Benefits are paid on a specific day each month, depending on your state.

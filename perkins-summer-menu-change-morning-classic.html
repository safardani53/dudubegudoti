Perkins makes summer menu change and customers will love the fresh take a morning classic

PERKINS Restaurant & Bakery is bringing back its Bakery Inspired breakfasts for a brief run this summer.

Founded in 1958, the casual dining chain and bakery has over 250 franchise locations scattered across the nation.

The new meals will fuse standard breakfast fare with the brand's bakery specialties like Bavarian cream and chocolate icing.

“Our Bakery Inspired Breakfasts takes two of the things that we do best, breakfast and bakery, and combine them into delicious offerings that guests may indulge in morning, noon, or night,” said Diana Garcia-Lorenzana, Vice President of Marketing for Perkins Restaurant & Bakery.

The bakery-breakfast fusion items are:

  • Blueberry Muffin French Toast Platter
  • Boston Cream Pie Pancake Platter
  • Cinny’Min French Toast Platter
  • Lemon Blueberry Waffle Platter
  • Sticky Bun Waffle Platter

The menu will be available while supplies last through September 5.

Each platter will cost $8.99 at participating locations.

Additionally, Perkins announced new summer treats.

The 'Pineapple Upside Down Mammoth Muffin' and lemon meringue pie will now be available at the bakery.

MORE NEW FOODS

While Perkins focuses on combining breakfast and bakery goods, another chain is fusing Pepsi, pineapple, and pizza.

Little Ceasers announced that it will partner with Pepsi on the new

Pineapple Pizza Combo.

The new combo will bring back the previously discontinued Pineapple Pepsi exclusively at Little Ceasers.

As part of the combo, customers get a 16-ounce can of pineapple Pepsi and a large Pizza for just $9.99.

The pizza can include up to two toppings, including the very controversial pineapple.

"There's always a passionate discussion surrounding pineapple on pizza," said Greg Hamilton, Chief Marketing Officer, Little Caesars.

"Together with Pepsi, we're satisfying ALL pineapple lovers, regardless of how they feel about it as a pizza topping," he continued.

Pepsi also celebrated the flavor merger.

"Little Caesars fans appreciate great flavor and whether they like pineapple on their pizza or not, we're confident Pepsi Pineapple will be unapologetically enjoyed by their customers everywhere," said Scott Finlow, Chief Marketing Officer of PepsiCo Global Foodservice.

The Pineapple Pizza Combo will become available for order exclusively online for a limited time starting on July 17 while supplies last.

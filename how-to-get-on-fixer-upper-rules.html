How to get on Fixer Upper: Homeowner requirements for HGTV show

FIXER UPPER is a popular HGTV show where Chip and Joanna Gaines flip dilapidated houses.

Over the years, Chip and Joanna have flipped several houses, and now fans want to know how they can get on the show.

How can I get on HGTV's Fixer Upper?

Fixer Upper fans often see Chip and Joanna giving one lucky homeowner the house of their dreams.

While it might seem like couples are chosen at random, there are some strict requirements that homeowners must meet in order to participate.

Some of those rules, according to Country Living, include:

  • Homeowners must live within a 30-mile radius of Waco, Texas
  • Homeowners must have a minimum budget of $50,000 for renovations
  • Homeowners must already have a home ready to renovate
  • Homeowners must moveout during filming
  • Homeowners must be 21 or older

Do homeowners get to keep the furniture?

Along with fixing up homes, Joanna also serves as an interior designer and often brings in new furniture from the Gaines' Magnolia collection.

While these eye-popping pieces might appear to come with the house, homeowners actually have to purchase them.

After the show wraps, HGTV provides homeowners with an itemized list of the staging items and they are able to pick and choose what they want to keep at a discounted price, according to Country Living.

While the furniture might not come with the house, Joanna does throw in one big ticket item, such as a sectional or dining room table, free of charge.

How can I watch HGTV's Fixer Upper?

Fixer Upper first premiered on HGTV back in 2013 and was on-air for five years before production wrapped.

After the show ended, Chip and Joanna focused on launching their Magnolia brand, which has included a product line at Target, a realtor company, and a new television network, among other things.

While the show might have ended in 2018, Chip and Joanna returned to screens in 2021 for Fixer Upper: Welcome Home and then again in 2022 for Fixer Upper: The Castle.

All three shows are available to stream on HBO Max, Philo, and Discovery+ with active subscriptions.

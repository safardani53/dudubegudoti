Jason Khalipa: Early Life 

The star, Jason Khalipa, was born in San Jose, California, United States of America, on October 2, 1985. He used to play football as a nose guard in high school. In addition to shotput, the athlete competed in track and field.

Jason has always been sporty and active since he was a child who grew even more with years.

Educational Background 

Jason went to Archbishop Mitty High School. There the athlete participated in a variety of sports. At the same time, he worked part-time at Milipitas Health and Fitness as a front desk representative.

Likewise, after graduating from high school, Jason later attended Santa Clara University in Santa Clara, California. Surprisingly, he did not participate in any of the sports organized by his university. Instead, the athlete returned to full-time employment at Milipitas Health and Fitness.

For his first CrossFit competition, he trained from the same gym. He also earned a Bachelor of Arts in Business Management from college in 2007.

Daughter’s Deteriorating Health

Khalipa’s daughter Ava was diagnosed with leukemia in 2016. Ava was just four years old when she was first diagnosed.

As their daughter fought to overcome this condition, Jason and his wife Ashley stood by her side with unshakable positivity and strength. As a matter of fact, the athlete gave up competitive CrossFit to focus on his daughter.

Following Ava’s final treatment in 2018, the couple has devoted their efforts to raising awareness for children with cancer.

In fact, the two teamed up with the NEGU team to assist families in similar situations. Ava’s Kitchen, an annual charity event, was also started by them. They also organized the west coast’s largest non-affiliated blood drive.

Jason Khalipa: Professional Career

In 2008, Khalipa began his professional fitness career. Before his retirement, the athlete competed in eight CrossFit Games tournaments.

In the 2008 Championship, he won his first CrossFit game and then qualified for the 2009 CrossFit Games as a champion. Jason came in fifth place that year. However, the athlete did receive the title of “Spitiy of the Games.”

Following that, Jason continued to take first place in the CrossFit games from 2010 through 2015. He was also the World Champion on one occasion.

In 2015, the athlete competed in a team competition with his NorCol Crossfit unit. Jason returned for training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in 2016. That year, he won the US Open Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu competition in the adult 208 lb division. 

Following that, in 2017, the athlete competed in the 2017 CrossFit Games for a short time. But, after one tournament, he withdrew.

Other Endeavours

The athlete built his first gym, CrossFit Santa Clara, after winning the first CrossFit games in 2008. In 2011, he changed the name of the gym to NorCla CrossFit. As an affiliate of the NorCla, he even competed in team CrossFit games.

The gym was renamed again NCFIT in 2017. Jason also announced the gym’s disaffiliation from the CrossFit brand later in 2020.

As the athlete’s fame in the fitness world expanded, he published “As Many Reps As Possible” in 2019. This book chronicled his career as a fitness athlete and as an integral part of the CrossFit Games winner.

Jason is also the host of the podcast AMRAP Mentality and Business of Fitness. In addition, the athlete is a cancer ambassador for children.

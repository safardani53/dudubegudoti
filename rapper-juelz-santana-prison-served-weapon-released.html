Why was rapper Juelz Santana in prison and how much time did he get?

RAPPER Juelz Santana was in prison for weapons charges and served nearly 19 out of his 27-month sentence.

Santana had pleaded guilty to attempting to bring a loaded gun on a flight leaving New Jersey’s Newark Airport. 

Here is more on the charges.

Who is Juelz Santana? 

LaRon Louis James, better known as, Juelz Santana, 38, is an American rapper.

He is best known for being on Cam'ron's 2002 singles, There It Go (The Whistle Song), Oh Boy, and collaborating with Chris Brown on Run It!

Santana is a member of the East Coast’s hip-hop group, The Diplomats.

He has also appeared in reality TV’s Love & Hip Hop: New York in its ninth season.

Why was Juelz Santana in prison?

Santana went to prison after airport TSA officers scanned his bag via X-ray and suspected a weapon was in it. 

When he approached the security checkpoint at New Jersey’s Newark Airport, he had asked TSA to search him “expeditiously” or else he would miss his flight to San Francisco, according to authorities.

That is when TSA scanned his bag and suspected the .38-caliber loaded weapon was present, cops say.

Santana left the airport in a taxi and was later located, as he had left his boarding pass, two other bags and driver’s license behind.

How much time did he get?

Santana served 19 out of the 27-month sentence he was given. 

He pleaded guilty to possession of a firearm by a convicted felon and carrying a weapon on an aircraft, according to the office of US Attorney Craig Carpenito.

Part of his deal included handing over the weapon to the government and two rounds of ammunition.

When did he go to jail?

Santana’s sentencing was on December 12, 2018, but was allowed by a judge to carry it out after performing for the rest of The Diplomats’ tour.

He officially went to jail on March 1, 2019. 

Santana served his time at Petersburg Medium Federal Correctional Institution in Hopewell, Virginia. 

Santana also had a run-in with the law in 2011 and pleaded guilty to distributing marijuana.

He had also faced charges for violating a restraining order in two prior incidents.

Santana was released from jail on August 5.

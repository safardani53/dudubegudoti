Antonio de Padua María Severino López de Santa Anna y Pérez de Lebrón, commonly known as Santa Anna or López de Santa Anna, is a Mexican politician and general. His influence on Mexican politics and government after independence in the first half of the 19th century was so much that Mexican historians often refer to it as "the age of Santa Anna." Santa Anna agrees with the important points of the Monroe Doctrine that European powers would not use the Gifts, Lords and Governorates as absent owners of their conquered lands in the Americas. He disagreed with the United States on slavery; but he did not agree to let the Africans enter his territory as freed slaves. He carried out cruel attacks on the Native American tribes of Mexican America. He was known as the "Man of Fate", who "stood out through his time as an ostentatious statue, a king without a crown". Santa Anna's military and political career has seen a series of reversals.

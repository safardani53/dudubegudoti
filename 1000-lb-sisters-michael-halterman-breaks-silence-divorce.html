1000-Lb. Sisters star Amy Slaton's ex Michael Halterman breaks silence after divorce in rare new pos

1000-LB Sisters star Amy Slaton's ex-Michael Halterman has broken his silence in a rare post amid the pair's divorce.

The reality star took to his Instagram Stories on Sunday to share how he was spending his Easter holiday.

In it, Michael, 40, shared a close-up shot of his youngest son, Glenn, during what appeared to be snack time.

The nine-month-old sported shorts and no shirt while sitting in his stroller.

He appeared to enjoy his dessert as he had what looked like chocolate smeared all around his mouth, chest, stomach, hands, and feet.

Michael gushed over the tot and his two-year-old brother, Gage, in his caption: "Happy Easter with my two beautiful boys."

The post was the first that Michael shared following the news of his split from Amy, 35, last month.

Though the TV star doesn't post much on the platform, he definitely won't be posting anything about his feud with Amy, per court orders.

Earlier this month, The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed that the exes are not allowed to make public statements about one another as the court proceedings continue.

According to the court documents exclusively obtained by The U.S. Sun, the judge has ordered Amy and Michael to "not make any public statements or social media postings concerning this litigation or one another."

Amy has not mentioned her divorce on her social media accounts but has stated that she is a single mom in her TikTok bio.

She previously posted a photo of herself and Michael holding their sons at her sister Tammy's wedding.

The TLC star captioned the photo: "Tammy and Caleb wedding November 19, 2022," and paired the picture with the song "I Love You, Baby" by Surf Mesa.

Amy wrote: "A pic of my little family at Tammy's wedding."

COURT ORDERS

In addition to banning Amy and Michael from posting about their divorce on social media, the court has ordered Michael to move out of their shared $37,000 two-bedroom home.

The U.S. Sun previously reported the court ruled Michael "must remove all clothing, paperwork, and personal effects" from the home.

Michael will be permitted to enter their marital home "at an agreed-upon time and date."

The court has allowed Michael to enter the residence "to recover his clothing, paperwork, and personal effects."

Amy is allowed to have a third party supervise Michael's short and formal visit.

KEEPING THEIR DISTANCE

The court also ruled that Michael and Amy must "remain 500 feet from one another at all times."

They are directed to "remain at least 500 feet" from each other's properties, and all communication must be made on an app supervised by the court.

The ex 1000-Lb. Sisters couple must also "not make any public statements or social media postings concerning this litigation or one another."

Lastly, Amy and Michael must file their 2022 taxes separately, with each "permitted to claim one minor child and each being solely responsible for any resulting refund of deficiency." 

The U.S. Sun previously reported the judge ruled Amy and Michael will have temporary joint custody of their two sons.

According to the court documents, Michael believes he deserves joint custody as he and Amy have "been in a caregiver role for the children since birth."

As decided on March 22, the court must enter a "shared parenting schedule," which will allow Amy and Michael to "maximize the amount of available parenting time."

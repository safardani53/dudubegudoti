Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce's Date Night Restaurants

Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce have great taste in restaurants.

The pop icon and football star’s relationship means the two have dine at a variety of eateries across New York City and Kansas City, where Kelce plays for the Kansas City Chiefs.

A source told PEOPLE that they are giving romance "a real try," despite both of their schedules being "actually insane."

“When you say a relationship is public, that means I’m going to see him do what he loves, we’re showing up for each other, other people are there and we don’t care,” Swift said of their relationship in her Time Person of the Year interview.

“The opposite of that is you have to go to an extreme amount of effort to make sure no one knows that you’re seeing someone," she added. "And we’re just proud of each other,"

From a Saturday Night Live after-party to a more intimate sushi meal, here are the details on their chic dinner dates.

Miracle

The pair have been spotted at a Kansas City pop-up bar called Miracle twice — one time during a holiday party with Brittany and Patrick Mahomes and another on Dec. 10 following the Chiefs game against the Buffalo Bills. The spot, which has three locations in Kansas City, is a limited-run holiday-themed bar with 131 locations across the U.S., so fans can have a chance to visit their local Miracle. A sampling of fun drinks on the menu include the cognac-based “Jingle Balls Nog," the vodka-based “Rudolph’s Replacement" and the vodka-based “Christmapolitan,” which is the one that fans on X speculated Swift drank.

Piropos

On Oct. 23, a day after Swift watched the Kansas City Chiefs take on the Los Angeles Chargers, the songstress and tight end shared dinner at Piropos in Missouri, according to photos obtained by The Daily Mail. The couple arrived at the restaurant in Kelce's Rolls Royce. The Argentinean steakhouse, which reportedly stayed open later than usual for the couple, per The Daily Mail, features big cuts of meat and seafood served with salt and spices imported from Argentina — as well as an array of empanadas.

The Waverly Inn

Swift and Kelce were spotted walking hand-in-hand leaving the intimate Greenwich Village restaurant on Oct. 15. The menu of the bespoke restaurant is composed of classic American dishes like Baltimore-style crab cakes, a cheeseburger, a wedge salad, burrata and more. The Waverly Inn’s luxe interior features large, red-leather chairs and banquettes and an impressive fireplace in the garden.

Catch Steak

The two enjoyed their time together at an afterparty at Catch Steak following the season 49 premiere of SNL in New York City on Oct. 14. A source tells PEOPLE the two “were so smiley, they were so happy,” at the dinner, taking time to chat with everyone around them. The classic New York City steakhouse serves traditional dishes like big cuts of steak, lobster and an array of sauces, appetizers and sides. The source tells PEOPLE that the couple was, “going back and forth about what to order, like, ‘Should we get a steak? Should we should get like espresso martinis?’”

Nobu

Prior to showing up on SNL, the power couple dined at Nobu 57, the Midtown Manhattan location of the popular sushi destination. A source tells PEOPLE that Swift and Kelce dined in the private hibachi room with eight other guests. The restaurant, which has locations around the globe, is known for signature dishes like its miso black cod, yellowtail and jalapeno appetizer, and their take on spicy tuna with crispy rice. The source added that Kelce frequents Nobu, usually opting for the toro special.

Career

Mezzanotte made her debut at the Sanremo Music Festival in 1990 with her song Sarai Grande, finishing fourth in the Newcomers category. She continued to collaborate with artists such as Francesco De Gregori, Mia Martini, and Laura Pausini. She took over as lead singer of the famed band Matia Bazar in 1999, replacing Laura Valente; with them, she participated in three subsequent editions of the Sanremo Music Festival: in 2000 (with Brivido caldo, reaching eighth place), 2001 (with Questa nostra grande storia d’amore, ranked #3), and 2002, this time winning the contest with Messaggio d’amore, before embarking on a two-year world tour.

Silvia formally rejoined Matia Bazar in September 2010, and the group’s new song, Gli occhi caldi di Sylvie, was released soon after. Matia Bazar returned to the Sanremo Music Festival in 2012, however, their song Sei tu failed to qualify for the final. Tragically, drummer and founding member Giancarlo Golzi died of a heart attack in August 2015, during the group’s 40th anniversary tour. Silvia left the group again in February 2016, after a brief absence. She revealed her impending new album, 5.0, in 2017; her fourth solo album, Aspetta un attimo, was published in 2019. Mezzanotte has been teaching singing in her Mazara del Vallo school, The Vocal Academy, since 2015.
